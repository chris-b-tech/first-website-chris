3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency

Benefits:
There are many benefits to using IT for productivity and efficiency.
IT allows us to connect to the internet and research, connect, upload and download files and many other things.
This allows us to use different tools for different projects. For me I used Studio Visual Code, Trello and some research online.
All of this was easily accessible right from my home, which meant I could work on my website at any time of the day without any issues and setbacks.
This led to me being more productive and efficient because I could easily research some website designs or some code that I was stuck on in no time.
It also allows me to communicate with my instructors in real time if I had any issues using slack.

Drawbacks:
Some drawbacks with using IT is that errors do occur.
Maybe a program you need to work has stopped working or the network is having issues so you can save your work to the server.
These can cause setbacks in a project as you may need to restart a whole days worth of work because of a setback.
Using a different pc or laptop can also be a huge setback, what if it doesnt have the necessary tools you need installed.
This can cause a lot of time wasted in installing software needed to be productive.
But I do think the benefits do outweigh the drawbacks. S without IT some things may be impossible to accomplish.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

Track and limit how much time you're spending on tasks.
Giving yourself a time limiton various tasks.
Spending too long on a task can actually become counter productive.
There are tools that can help by letting you know exactly how much time you spend on daily tasks.
Take regular breaks.
It sounds counterintuitive, but taking scheduled breaks can actually help improve your concentration.
Taking short breaks during long tasks helps you to maintain a constant level of performance..
Set self-imposed deadlines.
Giving yourself a deadline can also help.
You may be surprised to discover just how focused and productive you can be when you're watching the clock.
Quit multitasking.
Make a habit of committing to a single task before moving on to your next project.
Otherwise you may find yourself with too many tasks to do and may lose focus on all off them
Take exercise breaks.
Getting your blood pumping could be just what's needed to clear your head and get your focus back.
Giving yourself time to think away from the screen/phone etc.
Turn off notifications.
Turn off notifications will allow you to stay focused on the task at hand and not get distracted by messages on facebook or instagram etc.

3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions

I would research the digital problem first to get an insight as to what is going on.
This can give me an insight on a solution.
After some research, I will then try and apply what I learnt into action.
The error could be as small as a missing file perhaps, or in visual code a simple syntax error.
In this case it would be a quick fix to the problem.
But in more serious cases it could be, maybe the program is receiving errors or will not run.
In this situation I would first make sure it is up to date.
If this doesn't solve the issue I would reinstall the program.
Then I would make sure there aren't any driver conflicts and they are up to date.
I would do a lot of trial and error to find a solution and record what I have done up until that point.
This will allow me to see what has or hasn't worked and will help me in finding the solution quicker in the future.
